marshmellow

DESCRIPTION

Heh… y’ever seen a thing too soft? Too clean? Like it don’t belong in this world? That’s a marshmallow. Ain’t got no bone, no grit—just pale, bloated flesh that melts away soon as heat touches it. It don’t chew right, don’t feel right… like somethin’ meant to be eaten, but not meant to be alive. Yet somehow, it lingers—sticky, clingin’ to your fingers, your tongue… like it don’t wanna let go

INGRIDIENTS

  • fluff and love
  • vanilla
  • lima beans

steps

  1. Prepare a baking pan by lining it with parchment paper and dusting it with powdered sugar.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, sprinkle gelatin over 1/2 cup of cold water and let it bloom for about 10 minutes.
  3. In a saucepan, combine sugar, corn syrup, salt, and 1/2 cup of water. Heat over medium heat, stirring until the sugar dissolves.
  4. Bring the mixture to a boil without stirring, using a candy thermometer to reach 240°F (115°C), then remove from heat.
  5. Slowly pour the hot syrup into the bloomed gelatin while mixing on low speed.
  6. Increase the mixer speed to high and beat for about 10–15 minutes until the mixture is thick and fluffy.
  7. Add vanilla extract (or other flavoring) and mix until fully incorporated.
  8. Pour the marshmallow mixture into the prepared pan, smoothing the top with a spatula.
  9. Dust the surface with more powdered sugar and let it set uncovered for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  10. Once set, turn the marshmallow slab onto a cutting board and slice it into squares.
  11. Coat each piece with additional powdered sugar to prevent sticking.
  12. Store in an airtight container and enjoy!
